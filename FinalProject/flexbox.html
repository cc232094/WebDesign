Facts

Fun Fact 1

Completely Public

All 363 miles of coastline are completely accessible to the public, meaning anyone is free to visit any part of the Oregon Coast thanks to the 1967 Oregon Beach Bill. Signed by Tom McCall, it designated the land from the water up to 16 vertical feet above the low tide mark available to the public, protecting it from the encroaching private developments.

Fun Fact 2

Highway 101

The famous Highway 101 is a highway that spans the entire stretch of the Oregon Coast and more, running from Los Angeles, California to Olympia, Washington. This highway has the Pacific Ocean on its west side and the actual Oregon coast on its east side. This makes driving down the highway a great way to admire the beauty of the coast in its entirety.

Fun Fact 3

Sections of The Coast

The Oregon Coast is divided up into three sections: the northern coast, known for its tourism and being home to the oldest American settlement west of the Rocky Mountains, the central coast, which is a bit more rugged and acts as a bit of a transition between north and south, and the southern coast, known for its more remote and rocky scenery accompanied by cliffs.
